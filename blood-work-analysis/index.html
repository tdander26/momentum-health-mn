The part most people never get

Three People, the Same "Normal" TSH, Three Different Problems

Say three patients walk in exhausted, and all three have a TSH sitting inside the normal range. A panel that stops at TSH calls all three of them fine. They are not fine, and they do not have the same problem.

Every finding traces back: the symptom you feel → the dysfunction behind it → the actual driver → the correction. That last step is the whole point.
1️⃣

Signal Problem

The thyroid itself is fine, but the signal coming from the pituitary is off. Nothing on a TSH-only panel explains why this person is dragging.

2️⃣

Autoimmune

Antibodies are quietly attacking the gland. That is Hashimoto's, and it often shows up in antibodies years before TSH ever budges. You only find it if you run them. More on Hashimoto's →

3️⃣

Conversion

Plenty of T4 gets made, but it never converts to the active T3 your cells need. Free T3 and reverse T3 tell that story. TSH will not. More on thyroid care →

What People Ask About Blood Work

The questions we hear most from new patients in Elk River and the surrounding area.

My doctor said my labs are normal. Why would yours be any different? +
Same numbers, different ranges. A standard lab range is wide enough to catch disease, so plenty of room exists inside it to feel terrible. We compare your results to functional ranges, the tighter window where people actually feel well, and we read markers as patterns instead of one line at a time. That is usually where the answer has been hiding.
What does it cost? +
$216.30 for the panel plus an $8 draw fee. That is our cost passed straight to you with no markup, which is unusual. Running this same list of markers retail runs $750 and up, so you keep more than $530. We also take HSA and FSA.
What is actually included? +
Over 60 biomarkers: complete blood count with differential and platelets, a full metabolic panel, lipids, a complete thyroid panel (TSH, T4, T3, free T3 and free T4, reverse T3, TPO and thyroglobulin antibodies, TBG), iron studies (iron, TIBC, ferritin, saturation), vitamin D, homocysteine, hs-CRP, magnesium, hemoglobin A1C, insulin, and ANA for an autoimmune screen.
Can you use blood work I already had done? +
Bring it in! If it is recent and reasonably complete we are happy to go through it with you. Most standard panels are missing the thyroid and inflammation pieces we lean on, so we may still recommend filling in the gaps. But there is no reason to redo work you already paid for.
Do I need to fast? +
Yes, at least 8 hours, water only. No coffee, not even black. Also skip biotin for 3 days beforehand because it throws off several results. Morning draws are easiest for most people. Full instructions here →
Do you take insurance? +
We do not bill insurance. We provide a superbill you can submit for possible reimbursement depending on your benefits, and we accept HSA and FSA. Part of why we pass the panel through at cost is to keep the whole thing reasonable without insurance in the middle.
