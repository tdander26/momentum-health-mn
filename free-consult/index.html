Common Questions

What to Expect

A few things people usually ask before booking.

Is a consultation required before booking? +
No — consultations are optional. If you have questions before deciding to schedule, this is the perfect opportunity to get them answered. There's no obligation to book; feel free to use the time however you need.
Do you take insurance? +
We do not take insurance. However, we do provide a superbill so your insurance company can reimburse you directly depending on your benefits. Our rates are time-based — not driven by an insurance menu — which means everything we do together is because it's what we genuinely believe is best for you. We also happily accept HSA and FSA accounts.
Is blood work required before my first visit? +
No. Blood work can help find the root cause of your problems, but the doctor can often move forward without it depending on your situation and budget. During your first visit, he'll discuss whether it makes sense for you.
What conditions do you work with? +
We work with a wide range of chronic conditions — thyroid dysfunction, gut issues (IBS, leaky gut, SIBO), hormonal imbalances, autoimmunity, chronic fatigue, chronic pain, fibromyalgia, insulin resistance, anxiety/depression, and more. If you've been told your labs are "normal" but still feel terrible, that's exactly who we help.
How do you address nutritional issues? +
The first visit is used to go through your full history and any existing blood work to develop a list of probable root issues. From there, we narrow the search and build a treatment plan specific to you — not a generic protocol.
I need to get in as soon as possible. What are my options? +
Priority appointments are available for new patients who can't wait. These earlier slots are available for an additional fee (roughly 1.5x the standard rate) for the duration of the wait time skipped, then revert to standard pricing.
