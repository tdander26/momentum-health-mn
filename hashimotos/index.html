Why Most Hashimoto's Care Falls Short

Replacing the Hormone Doesn't Stop the Attack

Levothyroxine replaces what the thyroid can't make — which is helpful and necessary. But it does nothing about why the immune system is attacking the gland in the first place. The drivers (gut barrier, food triggers, infections, stress, blood sugar) keep working in the background until they're addressed.

The thyroid example: the symptom (weight gain, fatigue) traces back through dysfunction → driver → correction. We find the driver, not just manage the symptom.
🔍

Find

Full thyroid panel with antibodies (TPO and TG), plus the typical autoimmune drivers: gut barrier, food sensitivities, infections, blood sugar, stress, and key nutrients (selenium, zinc, vitamin D). Combined with a complete history and Applied Kinesiology — the goal is to find your Hashimoto's driver.

🛠

Fix

Targeted at the autoimmune side. Gluten is often non-negotiable for Hashimoto's; we'll explain why. Gut barrier work is almost always part of it. Stress and blood sugar work where indicated. Antibody numbers often come down meaningfully as the right drivers get treated.

Stabilize

A long-term Hashimoto's-friendly lifestyle: the few foods that consistently trigger your antibodies, stress and sleep, and a maintenance plan so flares get rarer and gentler.

Common Hashimoto's Questions

What People Ask About Hashimoto's

The questions we hear most often from new Hashimoto's patients in Elk River and the surrounding area.

Can Hashimoto's antibodies actually come down? +
Often, yes — sometimes significantly. We see TPO and TG drop as the upstream drivers (gut barrier, food triggers, stress, blood sugar) get addressed. No one can guarantee a number in advance, but for most patients the right work moves antibodies in the right direction.
Do I really have to be gluten-free with Hashimoto's? +
For most people with Hashimoto's, yes. The reason is molecular mimicry — the immune system that's attacking the thyroid is cross-reactive with gluten proteins. We'll walk through the why so it makes sense, not just feel like another rule.
I'm already on Synthroid / levothyroxine — can you still help? +
Yes. We don't change your medication — that stays between you and your prescribing doctor. We work on the autoimmune drivers in parallel, which often makes the medication work better and reduces the size of dose changes needed over time. See our general thyroid page →
Should I get my antibodies tested? +
Yes — if you have hypothyroid symptoms, a family history of autoimmunity, or a thyroid that's slowly drifting on labs. TPO and TG antibodies are the standard. A lot of people are running around with Hashimoto's and don't know it because no one ran the antibody panel.
Do you take insurance? +
We do not take insurance. We provide a superbill so your insurance can reimburse you directly depending on your benefits, and we accept HSA and FSA. Our rates are time-based, which means we do what's actually best for you — not what an insurance menu allows.
